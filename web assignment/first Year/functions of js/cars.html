FACTS ABOUT CARS

First Electric Car

The first electric-powered vehicle was created by Robert Anderson in 1832. Then in the 1990s, General Motors began mass-producing them. Today, drivers are gradually opting to buy an electric car to reduce their carbon footprint and impact on the environment.

Number of Cars

It is estimated that over 1 billion passenger cars travel the streets and roads of the world today. Out of this, over 6 million electric vehicles (EVs) were sold worldwide in 2021, according to a research done on the global electric vehicle market.

HISTORY OF CARS

Steam Machine of Verbiest

The first working steam-powered vehicle was designed and quite possibly built by Ferdinand Verbiest. It was a 65-centimetre-long (26 in) scale-model toy for the Kangxi Emperor that was unable to carry a driver or a passenger.

Gustave Trouvé's Tricycle

In November 1881, French inventor Gustave Trouvé demonstrated the first working (three-wheeled) car powered by electricity at the International Exposition of Electricity, Paris.

Ford Model T (1927)

Large-scale, production-line manufacturing of affordable cars was started by Ransom Olds in 1901 at his Oldsmobile factory in Lansing, Michigan and based upon stationary assembly line techniques pioneered by Marc Isambard Brunel at the Portsmouth Block Mills, England, in 1802.
